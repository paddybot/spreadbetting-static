Twitter shares fall 15% in post lockup sell-off

Analysts expected that Twitter co-founders Jack Dorsey and Evan Williams’ publicly stated intention not to offload shares would increase confidence in the stock. Venture capital firm, Benchmark, and Rizvi Traverse Management LLC, accounting for a combined stake of almost 20% in the business, also disclosed that they would not be selling shares, according to Bloomberg. However, the extent of the sell-off was clearly underestimated on Wall Street. Perceptions that the impending lockup expiration was already represented in the share price were flawed. Twitter shares have been in freefall over the first 5 months of 2014; the social media company has shed more than 50% of its value. Slowing user growth rates are widely thought to be the primary source of concern. Year on year growth rates are currently down 5.3%, despite improved Q1 growth versus 2013. Like Facebook, Twitter’s appeal is dependent on its ability to maintain and expand its active user base. Investors will no doubt be watching closely to see how the firm addresses this critical issue.
