Google moves to &#039;Titan&#039; grip on internet connectivity

In addition to offering good quality ubiquitous internet connectivity, Google purport the acquisition will add value to Maps initiatives also. Google and Titan spokespeople both alluded to the exciting potential this deal represents in statements today. Elsewhere, Google issued a limited number of Google Glass devices for general sale this morning. The search engine giant did not reveal how many pairs would be made available for purchase at this time, nor did they indicate how long the sale would last. Currently there are almost 10,000 such devices in circulation; Google hand-picked Explorers to try out the technology. Despite the projected price tag of approximately $1500, Glass is perhaps the most eagerly anticipated product of 2014. Class C GOOG shares (Google stock split) jumped 0.74% to close the day at $536.44, while Class A GOOGL stock also climbed a considerable 0.64%. Investor optimism surrounding Google’s foray into drone technology appears strong. US stocks rallied in Tuesday trading as the Dow, S&P 500 and NASDAQ all ended the day higher. Coca-Cola and J&J’s better-than-expected first quarter earnings were the springboard for today’s rebound; watch out for Google and IBM earnings announcements tomorrow.
