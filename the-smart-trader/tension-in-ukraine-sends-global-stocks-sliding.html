Tension in Ukraine sends global stocks sliding

Gold futures gained over 2% and crude oil jumped more than $2 per barrel, while top-rated euro zone government bonds were highly sought after amidst the political instability. Likewise gold and silver rose sharply, with price gains of 2.28% and 1.01% respectively for the day. Today’s scramble for safe havens is indicative of the general level of hesitation concerning Russia’s foray into Ukrainian territory. US stocks’ buoyant progress throughout last week was also hindered by the continued unrest in Ukraine. The S&P 500, which closed at a new record high on Friday evening, fell almost 0.75% Monday due to apprehensive investors’ reluctance to commit. The benchmark’s loss was its biggest in over a month. Both the Dow Jones and NASDAQ also lost less than a percent. Frederic Dickson, chief investment strategist at D.A Davidson and Co. alluded to the fact that ‘Global markets typically sell off on news of an escalated geopolitical crisis like we’re seeing in Ukraine’. Dickson noted that the extent of such a sell off will depend primarily on the ‘effectiveness of diplomacy’.
